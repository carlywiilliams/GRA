World's Best Tiramisu

The world’s best tiramisu is a heavenly balance of rich espresso-soaked ladyfingers layered with velvety mascarpone cream, dusted generously with cocoa for the perfect bitter-sweet finish. Each bite melts on the tongue, delivering a luxurious harmony of flavor and texture that feels both indulgent and timeless.

Ingredients

  • Ladyfingers (Savoiardi biscuits) – crisp, airy cookies that form the base.
  • Espresso – strong, freshly brewed coffee for soaking the ladyfingers.
  • Mascarpone cheese – rich and creamy Italian cheese for the filling.
    • Egg yolks – whipped with sugar to make a custard-like cream.
  • Granulated sugar – to sweeten the mascarpone mixture.
  • Heavy cream (optional) – often folded in for extra lightness.
    • Cocoa powder – sifted over the top for a slightly bitter contrast.
  • Dark rum or Marsala wine (optional) – for added depth and aroma.

Steps

  1. Brew espresso (or very strong coffee) and let it cool. Add a splash of rum or Marsala wine if you like.
  2. Whip egg yolks with sugar until pale and creamy, then gently fold in mascarpone cheese.
  3. Optional: Whip heavy cream and fold it into the mascarpone mixture for a lighter texture.
  4. Dip ladyfingers quickly into the cooled espresso—just a second or two so they don’t get soggy.
  5. Layer in a dish: spread mascarpone cream, then a layer of dipped ladyfingers, and repeat until full.
  6. Finish with mascarpone cream on top and dust generously with cocoa powder.
  7. Chill in the fridge at least 4–6 hours (overnight is best) so flavors blend beautifully.
