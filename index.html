Classic Tiramisu

Layers of delicate espresso-soaked ladyfingers embrace a cloud of velvety mascarpone cream, crowned with a dusting of rich cocoa. Tiramisu is more than dessert—it’s a symphony of coffee, chocolate, and cream that melts in your mouth and lingers in your memory.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup strong coffee (cooled, can also use espresso)
  • 3 tbsp coffee liqueur (optional, e.g., Kahlúa or Marsala wine)
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup mascarpone cheese (room temp.)
  • 1 pack ladyfinger cookies (about 24 of them)
  • Cocoa powder (for dusting)

Steps

  1. Make the Coffee Mixture: In a shallow bowl, mix the cooled coffee with coffee liqueur (if using). Set aside.
  2. Make the Cream Mixture: In a bowl, whisk together egg yolks and sugar until pale and creamy. In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream until soft peaks form. Add mascarpone cheese to the yolk mixture, then gently fold in the whipped cream.
  3. Assemble the Tiramisu: Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the coffee (just 1–2 seconds—don’t soak). Lay them in a single layer at the bottom of a dish. Spread half of the mascarpone cream mixture over the ladyfingers. Add another layer of dipped ladyfingers, then spread the rest of the cream on top.
  4. Chill: Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours (overnight is best).
  5. Serve: Before serving, dust the top generously with cocoa powder.
