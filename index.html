Classic Tiramisu

Layers of delicate espresso-soaked ladyfingers embrace a cloud of velvety mascarpone cream, crowned with a dusting of rich cocoa. Tiramisu is more than dessert—it’s a symphony of coffee, chocolate, and cream that melts in your mouth and lingers in your memory.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup strong coffee (cooled; espresso works great)
  • 3 tbsp coffee liqueur (optional; Kahlúa or Marsala)
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup mascarpone cheese (room temp.)
  • 1 pack ladyfinger cookies (about 24)
  • Cocoa powder (for dusting)

Steps

  1. Make the coffee mixture: In a shallow bowl, mix cooled coffee with liqueur (if using).
  2. Make the cream: Whisk yolks and sugar until pale. In another bowl, whip cream to soft peaks. Beat mascarpone into the yolk mixture, then gently fold in the whipped cream.
  3. Assemble: Quickly dip each ladyfinger (1–2 seconds) and lay a single layer in your dish. Spread half the cream, add another dipped layer, then spread the rest of the cream.
  4. Chill: Cover and refrigerate at least 4 hours (overnight is best).
  5. Serve: Dust generously with cocoa right before serving.
